What is the Beam?

The beam is a massive purple searchlight mounted on the roof of Golden 1 Center in downtown Sacramento. After every Kings home win, the beam fires straight up into the night sky — a column of purple light visible from miles away across the Sacramento Valley.

Technically, it's a high-powered Xenon sky tracker light with a purple gel filter, similar to the type used at Hollywood premieres. The beam sits on a custom mount on the arena's rooftop and is aimed directly upward. On a clear night, the beam can be seen from up to 10 miles away. It activates within minutes of the final buzzer and stays lit for several hours.

The tradition started during the 2022-23 season — the year the Kings ended a 16-year playoff drought, the longest in NBA history. The beam quickly became the most iconic post-game celebration in the league. Fans flood social media with "LIGHT THE BEAM" after every home win, and opposing teams' fans have started dreading the phrase.

The beam only activates for home wins. Road wins don't count. There is no beam for losses. You either light the beam or you don't.

About This Site

This site tracks every beam lighting since the tradition began. All data is pulled live from ESPN's API and updates every 30 seconds during games. You can browse every season since the beam's debut, see game-by-game results, team rankings, and how the Kings stack up against the rest of the NBA.
